<p><p>Carbon nanotubes (CNTs) are cylindrical nanostructures made of carbon atoms, known for their exceptional mechanical, electrical, and thermal properties. The market for CNTs is primarily divided into two types: single-walled carbon nanotubes (SWNTs) and multi-walled carbon nanotubes (MWNTs). SWNTs consist of a single layer of carbon atoms rolled into a tube and exhibit unique electronic properties, while MWNTs consist of multiple concentric tubes, offering enhanced mechanical strength and versatility. Both types have applications in electronics, materials science, and energy storage.</p></p>
