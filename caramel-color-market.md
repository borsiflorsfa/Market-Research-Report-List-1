<p><p>Caramel color is categorized into four classes based on its production process and characteristics. Class I is the lightest, made from sugar using minimal heat, suitable for clear beverages. Class II has a deeper color, often used in sauces and baked goods. Class III offers a stronger color and flavor, commonly found in dark sodas. Class IV is the most intense, produced under high heat, used in products requiring robust coloring, such as beer and distilled spirits. Each class serves specific applications in the food and beverage industry.</p></p>
