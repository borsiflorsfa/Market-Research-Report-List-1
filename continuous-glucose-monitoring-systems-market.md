<p><p>Continuous Glucose Monitoring Systems (CGMS) are categorized into wearable and implantable types based on their application and user experience. Wearable CGMS are external devices that attach to the skin and can be easily removed and reused, providing real-time glucose data through sensors. In contrast, implantable CGMS involve devices that are surgically placed under the skin for long-term glucose monitoring, offering prolonged use and minimized maintenance. Both types enhance diabetes management by providing continuous insights into blood sugar levels.</p></p>
