<p><ul><li>Inorganic Type</li><li>Organic Type</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The wood flame retardant market is primarily divided into two types: inorganic and organic. Inorganic flame retardants, such as metal hydroxides and phosphates, primarily rely on a release of water vapor upon heating to suppress flames and reduce smoke emissions. In contrast, organic flame retardants, including halogenated compounds and phosphorus-based materials, function through chemical reactions that interrupt combustion processes. Both types serve to enhance fire safety in wood products, catering to various applications and regulatory requirements in construction and manufacturing.</p></p>
