<p><ul><li>Sensible Heat Regenerative</li><li>Latent Heat Storage</li><li>Thermochemical Mode</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Thermal Energy Storage (TES) systems store excess thermal energy for later use, enhancing energy efficiency. The Sensible Heat market involves storing heat by raising the temperature of a medium, like water or rocks. Latent Heat Storage utilizes phase change materials that absorb or release energy during transitions (e.g., solid to liquid). Thermochemical storage relies on chemical reactions to store and release heat, allowing for higher energy density and longer storage durations. Each type serves different applications and efficiency needs in energy systems.</p></p>
